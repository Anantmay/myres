          Heavy Chinese missile, radar deployment near Ladakh but India ready to handle situation: Air chief Bhadauria

          ANI | Updated: Dec 29, 2020, 23:13 IST
          An IAF fighter jet flying in Leh
          NEW DELHI: The Chinese air force has conducted heavy deployment of missiles and radars in support of the aggression by their army along the eastern Ladakh sector, Air Chief Marshal RKS Bhadauria said on Tuesday. He added that India has taken all the required actions to handle the situation.
          Air chief Bhadauria was addressing a webinar on 'National Security Challenges and Air Power' organised by Vivekanand International Foundation.
          "China has deployed heavily along the Line of Actual Control (LAC) in support of their army. They have a large presence of radars, surface to air missiles and surface to surface missiles. Their deployment has been strong," he said.

          Pakistan a pawn in Chinese policy, serious conflict with India not good for Beijing: IAF chief


          The Chinese air force has deployed its indigenous fighter jets including J-20 and J-10 along with the Russian-origin SU-30 close to Indian territory in Tibet.
          They have also activated their air defence systems including the S-400 acquired from Russia.
          The Indian Air Force has deployed its frontline fighters including the Rafale and the MiG-29 in the Ladakh sector at multiple bases and is ready to respond to any misadventure by the Chinese side.
          On the counteraction from the Indian side, the IAF chief emphasised, "We have taken all actions required to be taken."

          India needs to enhance overall capability to deal with future security challenges: IAF Chief


          Bhadauria said the evolving uncertainties and instability at the global geopolitical front has provided China with an opportunity to demonstrate its growing power and indirectly it has also brought to fore the inadequate contribution of major powers to global security.
          The Air Chief said any serious India-China conflict is not good for China on the global front.
          "If Chinese aspirations are global then it does not suit their grand plan. What could be the possible Chinese objectives for their action in the north? It is important that we recognise what they have really achieved," he added.

          Cold wave conditions intensify in north India

          PTI | Dec 29, 2020, 21:32 IST
          Cold and foggy morning in Jammu. Cold wave conditions intensified across north India after some states receive...Read More
          NEW DELHI: Cold wave conditions intensified in north India on Tuesday with fresh snowfall reported from hill areas and Delhi recording a minimum temperature of 3.6 degrees celsius.
          According to the India Meteorological Department (IMD), a western disturbance led to "scattered to fairly widespread" snowfall in Jammu and Kashmir, Ladakh, Himachal Pradesh and Uttarakhand.
          Cold and dry northerly and northwesterly winds from the western Himalayas have been barrelling through the plains, bringing the minimum temperatures down in north India, the IMD said.
          Delhi reels under cold wave
          Delhi reeled under a cold wave as icy winds sweeping through the city brought the minimum temperature down to 3.6 degrees celsius.
          The Safdarjung Observatory, which provides representative data for the city, recorded a minimum temperature of 3.6 degrees celsius and a maximum of 18.1 degrees celsius, two notches below normal.
          The weather stations at Ayanagar and Lodhi Road recorded a low of 2.6 degrees celsius and 2.7 degrees celsius respectively.
          In the plains, the IMD declares a cold wave if the minimum temperature dips to 4 degrees celsius. A severe cold wave is when the minimum is 2 degrees Celsius or less.
          The IMD said the mercury may drop further by New Year's Eve.
          Kashmir records moderate snowfall
          Most places in Kashmir recorded moderate snowfall, bringing cheer to those associated with tourism and trade as they expect business to pick up by New Year's Eve.
          Officials said snowfall started in Srinagar around 7am, several hours after it had begun in neighbouring Budgam and Pulwama districts.
          The ski-resort of Gulmarg in north Kashmir recorded seven inches of fresh snowfall, while the Pahalgam resort in the south and the Sonamarg resort in central Kashmir received around three to four inches of snow each, they added.
          The snowfall ahead of the new year has seen many domestic tourists and locals rushing to Gulmarg and Pahalgam, the officials said.
          The minimum temperature in Srinagar -- the summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir -- was zero degree celsius on Monday night.
          The mercury settled at minus 3 degrees celsius in Pahalgam, minus 7.5 degrees celsius in Gulmarg, minus 0.4 degree celsius in Qazigund, minus 1 degree celsius in Kupwara and minus 3 degrees celsius in Kokernag.
          Gulmarg was the coldest recorded place in the valley.
          Cold wave in Himachal Pradesh
          Cold wave conditions intensified in Himachal Pradesh as minimum temperatures decreased by one to two notches, the IMD said.
          Keylong, Kalpa, Dalhousie and Kufri shivered at sub-zero temperatures, it added.
          Lahaul-Spiti's administrative centre Keylong remained the coldest place in the state at minus 10.3 degrees celsius, the IMD said, adding that it also received 0.6 mm snowfall.
          Kalpa in Kinnaur district recorded a low of minus 6 degrees celsius. The minimum temperatures in Dalhousie, Manali, Kufri, Seobagh, Solan and Bhuntar settled at minus 2.9, minus 2.6, minus 0.2, minus 1.5, minus 0.4 and minus 0.1 degree celsius respectively.
          Shimla recorded a low of 1.6 degrees celsius.
          Cold wave conditions in parts of Haryana and Punjab
          Cold wave conditions sweeping most parts of Haryana and Punjab for the past few days further intensified on Tuesday, with the minimum in Hisar settling at zero degree celsius.
          It was the season's coldest night so far at many places including Hisar, Narnaul, Amritsar and Chandigarh.
          The minimum in Haryana's Hisar dropped by six notches below normal, while Amritsar recorded 0.4 degrees celsius, officials said.
          Narnaul in Haryana also reeled under an intense chill, recording a low of 0.3 degrees celsius, five notches below normal.
          Karnal, Sirsa, Rohtak and Ambala recorded a minimum temperature of 2.6 degrees celsius, 2.5 degrees celsius, 2.6 degrees celsius and 3.5 degrees celsius respectively.
          Chandigarh, the common capital of the two states, also reeled under a severe chill, recording a low of 2.8 degrees celsius.
          Cold winds sweep through Rajasthan
          The maximum and minimum temperatures dropped at most places in Rajasthan as cold winds swept through the state, officials said.
          The state's only hill station, Mount Abu, recorded a low of minus 4 degrees celsius, followed by Sikar (minus 1 degree), Churu (zero degree), and Pilani and Bhilwara at 1 degree celsius each, they added.
          The Met department has issued a warning of severe cold in around a dozen districts of Jaipur, Bharatpur, Bikaner, Ajmer and Kota divisions of northeast Rajasthan till December 31.
          Dry weather in UP
          The weather remained dry in Uttar Pradesh with shallow to moderate fog recorded at isolated places in the last 24 hours, officials said.
          The day temperatures fell appreciably over Kanpur, Moradabad and Agra divisions but there were no major changes in the remaining divisions of the state.
          The lowest temperature in the state was recorded at Bareilly at 4.2 degrees celsius while the highest temperature was recorded at Prayagraj at 26 degrees celsius.
          Mumbaikars wake up to a chilly morning
          Mumbaikars woke up to a chilly morning on Tuesday as the minimum temperature dipped to 15 degrees celsius in the city, the lowest in the season, the IMD said.
          The trend of cool breeze and chill will continue for the next 48 hours, it added.
          The minimum temperatures dropped in other parts of Maharashtra as well. Nashik recorded a low of 11.8 degrees celsius, Pune 13 degrees celsius and Thane 17 degrees celsius.

          Experience Peak Performance with Kia Sonet on our drive to Maharashtra’s highest point - Kalsubai peak

          SPOTLIGHT | Dec 29, 2020, 12:38 IST
          Some say, no better way to shake off the ‘lockdown’ than to embark on an adventure. Only one way to find out...we head to one in the new Kia Sonet.
          After months of staying cooped up in one place, the world has just about started opening up. Obviously, heading out isn’t as simple as it used to be. There are new safety and hygiene standards to be maintained, and new precautions to be taken.
          Now, more than ever, a car is possibly one of the safest and easiest means to get out there. If you want a safe trip, it’s essential to have a suitable road-tripping companion too. So, when it came to our steed – the Kia Sonet was an obvious choice. And the tagline Wild by Design isn’t just a tagline for show; as we found out that in the best way possible with a drive to trekking hotspot Kalsubai - the highest peak in the state of Maharashtra.
          While an early start ensured little traffic on the roads out of town, once the highway opened up, we let the Sonet’s 1.0-litre turbo petrol engine flex its capable muscles. The 998cc heart churns out 120hp and 172Nm of spin; enough for the speedo to register three digits in the blink of an eye. Literally! The responsive steering, brilliant visibility in all directions from the driver’s seat and balanced brakes meant weaving through highway traffic was as easy as a hot knife through butter. And it saved so much time even with the added ritual of sanitising at regular intervals.

          EXPLORE BRIEFS
          Mehbooba Mufti alleges Centre disrespecting Constitution

          The former chief minister, who was an ally of the BJP from 2015 to 2018, said the mainstream political parties in Kashmir were working to achieve their goal of restoration of special status "within the Constitution of the country". She also accused the Centre of disrespecting the Constitution by repealing the special status of J&K.

          According to the data, the total sanctioned police forces in states and union territories is 26,23,225, while there are 20,91,488 police personnel actually in service. Therefore, 5,31,737 posts in police forces are lying vacant as on January 1, 2020.


          Speaking of hygiene and safety, having to breathe in truck exhaust fumes when you’re on the highway is something the Sonet addresses expertly. Its in-built Smart Air Purifier makes sure you breathe clean air, no matter the conditions outside. Especially relevant in these times, it makes sure the air inside is virus-free too. The Air Quality Index is displayed right on the central screen to make sure you know how healthy you are breathing, at all times.
          Even as our bellies growled with hunger, reminding us about a stop for breakfast, the Sonet showed no signs of needing any fuel top-up. There are advantages of having a 45-litre tank and a rated fuel economy of over 18 kilometres to the litre! No need for the customary air check either as the tyre pressure monitor made sure we knew that everything was in great shape.

          A long road trip, no matter the surrounding country and roads you traverse, needs some added entertainment. No shortage on that front. The premium Bose sound system made the music experience sublime. The UVO system, with its smartphone and smartwatch integration, made sure that we had a run of our entire playlist without ever having to take our eyes off the road or hands off the steering. The voice command recognition allowed us to map the route and displayed it on the massive 10.25-inch central touchscreen. OTA map updates ensured we remained on the right track at all times, even across the most remote of back roads. The wireless charging feature kept our phone juiced up to take on the hike without worries.
          But what’s a road trip without some fun detours? As we sped past the beautiful countryside, there were many trails that looked perfect for a bit of off-the-highway action. With its six different options for driving modes (three different drive modes and three traction modes), the Kia Sonet is armed to the teeth for uncharted territories. So, traction tuned to mud, we turned off the highway and decided to explore some of the interiors. Of course, a lot of the roads connecting the little villages en route were in a state of disrepair after the monsoon. That’s where the Sonet’s superb suspension came into play. Potholes, small or crater-size, were dispatched with absolute aplomb, sparing our backs any trouble whatsoever. And this made sure we didn’t have to slow down for any obstacles. When we did, with stray animals darting out onto the road, the progressive brakes made sure we managed it without a fuss.

          As we neared Kalsubai, there was yet another well-known attraction in the same region that draws a whole lot of visitors – Bhandardara Lake. With water looking superb and some places especially begging for us to stop for some photos, we ventured off the tarmac and literally to no roads. But with rocks large and small littering the shores of the lake, going was a bit tricky. This is when the proximity sensors really came in handy. It allowed us to make our way down to the water without a scratch and have had to step out to check progress.
          After getting Instagram-worthy shots, it was time to move and head to our ultimate destination. As the rugged peaks, renowned across the state for one of the most entertaining hiking routes, appeared in the distance, so did other hiking and adventure enthusiasts, all heading for a wildly exciting adventure. As other vehicles made progress a little slow, the Kia Sonet had no trouble negotiating traffic, with its commanding position behind the wheel making it all too easy.

          Soon, it was time to park ourselves at the little village of Bari at the base and start the climb. We unloaded our equipment and geared up, even as we fielded more questions about the Sonet than about our trek up. Given that there’s no parking lot proper and you leave your vehicle in the backyard of one of the many houses in the village, some might worry about finding it once they’re back from the exhilarating adventure. But no such issues with the Sonet; the UVO system, once again, would allow us to remotely locate the car through the Find My Car feature or the Remote horn/light function.
          It was time to head up for a ‘wild adventure’ nearly 200km in the making. We’d come a long way for an epic adventure and the Sonet had made sure we had been comfortable, safe and thoroughly entertained for the entire journey. There can be no better companion for exhilaration. So, until the next adventure!

          I don't hanker for CM's post, says Palaniswami

          PTI | Dec 29, 2020, 21:13 IST
          NAMAKKAL: Tamil Nadu chief minister K Palaniswami on Tuesday said he does not hanker for the post of CM and considers himself as someone who is "in charge" of executing the task that people assign to him.
          The AIADMK leader said that his role as chief minister was only to serve the people as what remained everlasting is one's good deeds.
          The chief minister, who launched "Vetrinadai Podum Tamizhagam" (Tamil Nadu leading the way), his poll campaign in Rasipuram in this district, said the people are the real chief ministers.
          "Earlier I was introduced as the chief minister. But I see everyone of you as chief minister. The people are the real chief ministers because you gave me this post which I accepted as in charge to serve you, to execute the task you assign to me."
          "Like others, I don't hanker for the chief minister's chair because as one hailing from a village I wish to work hard," Palaniswami said amid thunderous applause from party workers who had gathered in large numbers.
          He said he came up in life through sheer hard work.
          "I came up by working hard and thank our leaders (Late chief ministers) M G Ramachandran and J Jayalalithaa for this. What remains everlasting is our achievements."
          "This is what both our leaders have left behind through their good deeds," Palaniswami, whom the ruling AIADMK has announced as the chief ministerial candidate for the 2021 polls, said.
          He is also the AIADMK's co-coordinator.
          Training his guns against M K Stalin, the chief minister accused the DMK chief of spreading misinformation that AIADMK party men are involved in door-to-door distribution of tokens for collecting the Pongal gift of Rs 2,500.
          Stalin was spreading false information as he was unable to digest the fact that the Pongal cash gift scheme received an overwhelming response from the people, he alleged.
          His government had announced a Pongal cash gift of Rs 2,500 and hampers to all rice cardholders in the state.
          "He is doing so because the AIADMK will earn a good reputation among the people," he said.
          Last year, the DMK went to the court seeking to stall the distribution of Rs 1,000 cash to ration cardholders as that party disliked doing good things for the people, Palaniswami said, adding, he had announced Rs 2,500 cash gift to enable the affected people to celebrate Pongal happily.
          Ramachandran and Jayalalithaa always desired that poor people should live happily and hence the present AIADMK government is fulfilling their desire.
          Palaniswami, who earlier offered prayers at the Anjaneyar Temple in Namakkal, appealed to the people to cast their votes in favour of the Two Leaves, the AIADMK symbol, in the next year's assembly elections, due in April-May.
          Electricity minister P Thangamani, Transport minister M R Vijayabaskar and Social welfare minister Dr V Saroja and MLAs were also present.
